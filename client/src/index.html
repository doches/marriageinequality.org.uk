Since 2012, UK citizens earning less than £18,600 per year can only bring their non-EU partner into the UK by holding an amount of savings determined by:

£16,000 + 2.5 × (£18,600 - earnings)

Adult minimum wage in the UK is £6.50 per hour, which comes to at most £13,520 per year. At best, a UK citizen working full-time at minimum wage will need £28,700 in savings in order to bring his or her spouse to the UK. At 10% per month (almost double the average UK savings ratio), it would take 21 years to build up the required savings while living on only £1,000 per month.

And if you're unemployed or self-employed? You'll need the full £62,500 in savings.
